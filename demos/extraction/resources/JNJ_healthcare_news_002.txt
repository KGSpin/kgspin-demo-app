Title: FDA Approves Johnson & Johnson MedTech Device for Minimally Invasive Heart Valve Repair
Source: FDA Approvals
Category: device_approval
Published: Wed, 05 Feb 2026 08:30:00 EST

The FDA has approved a new transcatheter heart valve repair system developed by Johnson & Johnson's MedTech segment. The device, which integrates technology from the company's Abiomed acquisition, enables minimally invasive treatment of mitral valve regurgitation.

The approval is based on clinical data from a multi-center trial involving 800 patients across 45 hospitals. Results showed significant improvement in cardiac function with fewer complications compared to traditional surgical approaches.

Johnson & Johnson's MedTech division, which also incorporates Shockwave Medical's lithotripsy technology, now offers one of the most comprehensive cardiovascular device portfolios in the industry. The company competes with Abbott Laboratories and Medtronic in the structural heart market.

CEO Joaquin Duato called the approval "a milestone in our mission to transform cardiovascular care." The device is expected to generate approximately $1.2 billion in annual revenue once fully launched across U.S. and European markets.

The FDA noted that the streamlined review process reflected the agency's commitment to accelerating access to innovative medical technologies that address serious conditions.
