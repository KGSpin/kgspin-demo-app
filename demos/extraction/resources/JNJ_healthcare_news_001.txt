Title: FDA Grants Priority Review to Janssen's Novel CAR-T Therapy for Multiple Myeloma
Source: FDA News
Category: drug_approval
Published: Fri, 31 Jan 2026 10:00:00 EST

The Food and Drug Administration has granted priority review to a supplemental Biologics License Application for Johnson & Johnson's Janssen Biotech subsidiary for an expanded indication of its CAR-T cell therapy in relapsed or refractory multiple myeloma.

The FDA's decision to grant priority review reflects the therapy's potential to address a significant unmet medical need. The treatment, developed by Janssen's Innovative Medicine research division, showed a 74% overall response rate in the Phase 3 clinical trial.

Dr. Mathai Mammen, head of J&J's pharmaceutical R&D, noted that the therapy represents a breakthrough in cell-based cancer treatment. The company expects an FDA decision by mid-2026.

The priority review designation accelerates the standard FDA review timeline from approximately 12 months to 8 months. Johnson & Johnson competes with Pfizer and Bristol-Myers Squibb in the CAR-T therapy space.

This is the third priority review granted to a Janssen oncology product in the past year, underscoring the company's strengthening position in cancer therapeutics under CEO Joaquin Duato's leadership.
