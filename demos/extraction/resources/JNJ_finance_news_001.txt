Title: Johnson & Johnson Reports Strong Q4 2025 Earnings, Raises 2026 Guidance
Source: Financial Times
Published: Tue, 28 Jan 2026 14:30:00 +0000

Johnson & Johnson reported fourth-quarter 2025 earnings that exceeded Wall Street expectations, driven by robust growth in its Innovative Medicine and MedTech segments. The healthcare giant posted revenue of $22.5 billion, up 5.2% year-over-year.

CEO Joaquin Duato highlighted the company's strategic transformation following the Kenvue separation. "Our focused portfolio is delivering accelerating growth across both segments," Duato said during the earnings call. CFO Joseph Wolk noted that operating margins expanded by 120 basis points.

The Innovative Medicine segment, which includes the Janssen pharmaceutical portfolio, reported $13.8 billion in revenue. Key growth drivers included oncology treatments and immunology products. The MedTech division contributed $8.7 billion, boosted by the integration of Abiomed and Shockwave Medical acquisitions.

J&J raised its full-year 2026 guidance to $91-92 billion in revenue, above analyst consensus of $90.3 billion. The company also announced a 4.2% dividend increase, extending its streak as a Dividend Aristocrat.

Analysts at Goldman Sachs maintained their overweight rating, citing the company's strong pipeline of FDA approvals expected in 2026. JPMorgan Chase analysts noted that J&J's competitive position against Pfizer and Abbott Laboratories has strengthened following the consumer health spinoff.
