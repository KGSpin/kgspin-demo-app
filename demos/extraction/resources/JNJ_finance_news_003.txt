Title: UnitedHealth Group and Johnson & Johnson Partner on Value-Based Care Initiative
Source: Bloomberg
Published: Thu, 06 Feb 2026 11:45:00 +0000

UnitedHealth Group and Johnson & Johnson have announced a multi-year value-based care partnership focused on improving outcomes for patients with immunological conditions. The agreement covers several of J&J's Innovative Medicine products distributed through UnitedHealth's Optum pharmacy network.

The partnership reflects a broader industry trend toward outcomes-based pharmaceutical pricing. Joaquin Duato, J&J's CEO, described the deal as "a model for how pharmaceutical companies and payers can collaborate to improve patient access while ensuring value."

Janssen Biotech, the J&J subsidiary that manufactures the targeted therapies, will provide real-world evidence data to support the arrangement. The FDA-approved treatments covered by the agreement have demonstrated significant efficacy in clinical trials.

Goldman Sachs healthcare analysts noted that the partnership could serve as a template for similar arrangements with other major insurers. The deal is expected to increase J&J's Innovative Medicine segment revenue by an estimated $500 million annually.

Pfizer and Abbott Laboratories are reportedly exploring similar value-based arrangements with UnitedHealth Group.
